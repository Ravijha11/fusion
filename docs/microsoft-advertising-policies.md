# Microsoft Advertising Network Policies

This document serves as a reference for aligning the DigitalBull appliance repair website with Microsoft Advertising Network policies to ensure the site is highly optimized and ad-friendly.

Below is the compilation of the core advertising guidelines provided:

## 1. Landing Page Quality & Relevance
Landing pages should help users quickly find the products, services, or information relevant to the ad. Landing page and site content should exist to provide value.

**Prohibited Behaviors:**
- Using doorway pages or cloaking.
- Redirecting users unexpectedly to unrelated domains.
- Changing browser preferences or resetting home pages without consent.
- Resizing browser windows or disabling browser buttons.
- Delaying or obstructing user access to requested content by adding steps strictly meant to monetize the user.
- Featuring content designed primarily to support ad monetization, or sparse/limited content.
- Using pop-ups or pop-unders that are disruptive. (Expected experience functionalities like age verification or cookie consent are permitted).
- Spawning multiple pop-ups or any pop-up preventing the user from leaving the site.
- Misleading the user with fake dialog boxes, fake close behavior, or simulating a broken webpage.
- Sites requiring a sign-up/login must link back to a main home page detailing the services and terms of use.

## 2. Privacy & Personal Data
As an advertiser, it is your responsibility to comply with laws and regulations related to the collection and use of personal data.

**Requirements:**
- **Privacy Policy**: You must provide a prominent link from your landing page—or include within the landing page—a Privacy Policy.
- The Privacy Policy must:
  - Clearly and prominently include the name of the company (or site operator) collecting personal information.
  - Clearly state the purpose of the information being collected.
  - Provide opt-out instructions.
- Data collection must be secure (e.g., using SSL - `https://`).
- No ads driving to pages whose sole purpose is collecting personal data for promotional marketing.
- Do not require the entry of sensitive personal data unnecessary to provide the service or complete the purchase.

## 3. Transparency & Deceptive Content
- Ads and landing pages must be truthful, clear, and factual.
- **Refunds/Returns**: Refund and return policies must be easily accessible on your website.
- **Service/Product Availability**: Promoted products/services must be available in the targeted locations.
- Any claims associated must be realistic, substantiated, and not misleading. 
- Risks associated with products/services must be fairly disclosed (including necessary disclaimers).
- Ads cannot leverage misleading calls to action (CTAs). 

## 4. Financial & Checkout Security
- Payments must always be collected through a secure checkout process.
- Pricing information must be accurate. If services have minimum fees, the landing page must clearly display pricing accurately (e.g., minimum bulk quantities, hourly rates, or starting fees).

## 5. User Tracking & Remarketing
- If utilizing Universal Event Tracking (UET) tags for remarketing, the privacy policy must disclose that tracking and data sharing with third parties is taking place.
- Users must be instructed on how to exercise opt-out rights (linking to NAI or DAA opt-out pages).
- You cannot create remarketing lists based on sensitive data (health, religion, orientation, kids).
